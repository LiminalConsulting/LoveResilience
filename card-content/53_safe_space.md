# Meaning

This safe space — whether it's a physical place, a relationship, or simply a breath of quiet — doesn't have to be big or dramatic. Just somewhere you don't have to hold it all together. Somewhere you can soften.

It might be a corner of the couch, a person who sees you clearly, a few moments under the sky. Sometimes it's something you create for yourself when the world forgets to offer it.

There's nothing weak about needing that kind of space. It's where strength has room to stretch out and rest.

# Question

Where does safety live for you right now — and what might help you return to it when you've wandered too far from yourself?

# Action

Notice where you feel most like yourself — even if it's just a memory — and let that place be your anchor for today.

# Quote

> "The ache for home lives in all of us, the safe place where we can go as we are and not be questioned."
> — Maya Angelou
