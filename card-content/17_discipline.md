# Meaning

Resilience is not about sudden bursts of strength or action — it's about the quiet persistence of small efforts made consistently over time. Discipline is the framework that keeps us steady on our path. Even when the results aren't immediately visible, the daily practice, the small commitments, and the repeated actions gradually build our capacity to endure. Resilience grows in the moments when we show up, despite challenges, and continue putting in the work. It's the consistent effort that strengthens our resolve and leads us to success.

# Question

What small, consistent actions can you commit to today that will support your long-term resilience? Where can you apply a bit of discipline to build momentum and create lasting change?

# Action

Identify one area of your life where you've been inconsistent or lacking discipline. Today, set a specific, achievable goal for that area — it could be a task you've been putting off or a habit you've wanted to start. Break it down into smaller steps and commit to taking the first step today. Celebrate that small victory, knowing that resilience is built one step at a time. Remember, every step, no matter how small, is progress. Keep moving forward — you're capable of more than you think.

# Quote

> "Success is the sum of small efforts, repeated day in and day out."
> — Robert Collier
