# Meaning

Empathy, as Marshall Rosenberg teaches, is about truly listening and understanding the feelings and needs of others without judgment. When we embrace empathy, we are better able to connect with others' experiences and offer the kind of support that strengthens resilience. It's not about offering solutions right away or minimizing another's pain, but about creating a space where they feel heard and understood. This kind of empathetic connection builds resilience, because it reminds us that, even in the face of difficulties, we are not alone. Resilience grows when we can both offer and receive empathy, creating stronger, more compassionate bonds with others.

# Question

When you're experiencing challenges, how can you invite empathy into your situation? How might you show empathy for someone else who's struggling, or even for yourself as you move through tough emotions?

# Action

Today, practice empathy in your interactions — whether it's through active listening, asking open-ended questions, or simply being present with someone who needs to feel heard. If you're feeling challenged, turn to yourself with the same compassionate curiosity, seeking to understand what you truly need in this moment. Remember, resilience grows when we connect with both ourselves and others in a place of deep understanding.

# Quote

> "Empathy is a respectful understanding of what others are experiencing."
> — Marshall Rosenberg

Or

> "To listen is to lean in, softly, with a willingness to be changed by what we hear."
