# Meaning

Inside all of us lives a younger version — our inner child, who once saw the world with wide-eyed wonder and curiosity. Resilience is not about pushing this part of us aside. It's about listening to and honoring that inner child, even when life becomes overwhelming. Instead of shutting down in the face of adversity, we learn to channel the courage of our inner child — to take risks, feel deeply, and be unafraid to embrace both the chaos and beauty of life. Just as a ship faces storms yet keeps moving forward, our resilience is fueled by the same spirit of persistence and renewal.

# Question

What would your younger self want to say to you today? How can you make space to listen and offer them the comfort or encouragement they need?

# Action

**Action 1:** Close your eyes for a moment and imagine your younger self standing in front of you. What do they need right now? What would you say to them? Step into that role — be the protector, the cheerleader, the advocate they never had. Take a bold step towards your own healing today by honoring that need. It might be taking a break, speaking kindly to yourself, or letting go of an old fear.

**Action 2:** Take a moment to find something beautiful about yourself. It could be a strength you've forgotten, a talent you've never acknowledged, or even a simple act of kindness you've given to someone. Reflect on that beauty and let it fill you with gratitude. Reconnect with the part of you that is pure, untarnished, and resilient. Then, find a way to express that beauty today — whether through a small act, a compliment to yourself, or a creative outlet.

**Action 3:** Stand in front of a mirror and find something beautiful about yourself — maybe it's your smile. Acknowledge the strength in that smile, the warmth it gives to others, and the resilience it represents. Give yourself a moment to smile at yourself. See the power in that simple act. Allow that smile to remind you of your own resilience and to carry you forward with love and confidence today.
