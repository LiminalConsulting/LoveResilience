# Meaning

A sense of humor can feel like a deep breath in a storm — a shift in perspective, a softening of tension. It doesn't mean dismissing pain or pretending everything's fine. It means allowing space between you and what's weighing you down, so you can breathe, think, and even smile again. Humor helps you hold your experience with a lighter touch — not because it's not real, but because you're not letting it crush your spirit.

People who can laugh, even briefly, often show the deepest strength. They don't avoid the darkness — they simply don't let it own the whole room. Crying and laughing live close together in the body, and sometimes, one leads to the other. That's not weakness. That's release.

# Question

Can you remember a moment when you laughed — even in the middle of something difficult? What shifted in you in that moment? What becomes possible when you let lightness enter, without needing the situation to change?

# Action

Pause for a moment and imagine yourself as an older, wiser version of who you are now. From this perspective, what might you tell yourself about your current struggle? What would that future version remind you about resilience and the importance of laughter? Take a deep breath, step back, and allow yourself to see this moment with a sense of humor — not as a way to dismiss what's real, but to remind yourself that everything has its season, and even hard times eventually pass. In the grand story of your life, this moment is just a chapter — and you have the power to turn the page with a smile.

# Quote

> "I love people who make me laugh. I honestly think it's the thing I like most — to laugh. It cures a multitude of ills."
> — Audrey Hepburn
