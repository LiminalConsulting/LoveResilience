# Meaning

There are moments when life asks us to pause — not because we're tired, but because something deeper wants to be heard. In German, there's a beautiful word: innehalten — to hold within, to come to stillness for a breath, to step out of doing and into simply being.

This kind of pause is quiet but powerful. It lets us feel again, realign, and come home to ourselves. In a world that rushes us forward, this is how we return to the moment — and to what truly matters.

# Question

When was the last time you let yourself be still — not out of exhaustion, but out of intention? What might gently surface if you gave yourself permission to pause today?

# Action

Find one quiet moment today to pause — even if it's just 60 seconds. Let yourself feel the air, your breath, your heartbeat. Notice what's here, without needing to change it. If you like, place a hand on your heart or belly — a small gesture of returning to yourself. This isn't about solving or doing. Just being.
