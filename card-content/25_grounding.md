# Meaning

There are moments in life when everything around you shifts — plans fall apart, emotions rise, and the path ahead blurs. In those moments, what keeps you from losing yourself is not force, but grounding. Like the great oak that grows through centuries, your strength is not loud or fast. It is slow, quiet, and deep — anchored in what truly matters.

To be grounded means returning to your body, to the present moment, and to the earth that has held you since the beginning. It means remembering that you are part of something older, wiser, and more enduring than any passing storm. When you feel uncertain or lost, drop into that knowing. Let the ground remind you: you are not adrift — you are rooted.

# Question

Can you sense the quiet power within you — the part that doesn't need to push, but simply is? When things feel unstable, how might you return to that deeper sense of home inside yourself?

# Action

Step outside or sit somewhere still. Imagine yourself as an ancient oak. With each breath, feel your roots growing into the earth — strong, wide, reaching into layers of time and silence. Let your breath slow. Let your thoughts settle. Feel the support beneath you. Speak quietly to yourself: I am rooted. I am steady. I am enough. Let that be your anchor. Let the earth carry what you no longer need.

# Quote

> "Stand still. The trees ahead and the bushes beside you are not lost."
> — Anonymous (from a Native American teaching)
