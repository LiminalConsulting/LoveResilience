# Meaning

Sometimes resilience begins with something as quiet as the next inhale.

The breath doesn't demand. It doesn't rush. It simply arrives — again and again — reminding the body that it's safe to stay.

Not every moment can be fixed, but it can be felt. And the breath knows how to hold that — the ache, the tightness, the soft return.

It moves through the chest like a tide, smoothing out what's tangled, making space for you to stay upright, even in the middle of the storm.

# Question

Where in your body is your breath gently asking to be met — not shaped or forced, just noticed?

# Action

Take the biggest breath of your day so far — not to change anything, just to come home to yourself.
